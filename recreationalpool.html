Public

When at a public pool it is important to keep an eye on your surroundings. Don’t run, no food or drink on deck, and never - under any circumstances - get in the pool if there isn't a lifeguard on duty. Always be courteous of those around you, and make sure to have fun!

Home

If you have the luxury of being able to own your own pool, there are some things you should always keep in the back of your mind. Your pool, your responsibility. It's on you to make sure safety protocols are up to date and the chemicals are always balanced.

Diving

Most people are aware that it's extremely dangerous to dive in the shallow end of a pool. What many people are ignorant to believe, is that it is also dangerous to dive in the deep end if your form is wrong. There is an alarming amount of cases were people crash their skulls on the bottom of pools due to of improper form.

Equipment

Children who don't know how to swim require assistance. It is the responsibility of the parent or guardian, taking care of the child, to give them the proper equipment in order for them to stay safe. Things like: arm floaties, air tubes, foam noodles, and goggles.

Skin

A long time myth that has been passed around, "I don’t get sunburn, I don't need sunscreen" or "I burn first, then I get tan." Both these statements are completely false. Whether you get sunburn or not, your skin is still getting damaged if you are not applying sunscreen.
